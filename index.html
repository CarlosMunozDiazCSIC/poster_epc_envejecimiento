What is behind the geographical distribution of the elderly? Spatial patterns of the aging population in Spain

J. Sebastian Ruiz-Santacruz, Joaquín Osorio-Arjona, Clara Bueno López, Carlos Muñoz Díaz, Julia de las Obras-Loscertales Sampériz and Diego Ramiro Fariñas

The aging of Spain's population is the result of persistent declines in fertility rates, fewer children in number, and a sustained increase in life expectancy and survival to older ages that leads to a higher share of elderly people (age 65 years and older) in the population

Methods

  • - Description of geographical distribution of aging across Spain
  • - Geographically weighted regression (GWR)

Conclusions

  • - Low fertility levels and sustained declines in mortality are the primary drivers of the shift towards older ages
  • - GWR model proved to be a good model for analyzing the variables that affect the spatial distribution of the elderly

Future Work

  • - Incorporate information at a lower administrative level
  • - Compare with other models, such as spatial-lag or spatial error
